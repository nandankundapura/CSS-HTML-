The 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup was the tenth edition of the tournament and was co-hosted by India, Sri Lanka, and Bangladesh from 19 February to 2 April 2011. India, led by Mahendra Singh Dhoni, entered the tournament as one of the favorites, having a strong team featuring players like Sachin Tendulkar, Virat Kohli, Yuvraj Singh, and Zaheer Khan. The tournament featured 14 teams divided into two groups, and matches were played across multiple venues in the host countries. India progressed steadily through the group stage and knockout rounds, showing a mix of aggressive batting, disciplined bowling, and strategic fielding. The final match was held on 2 April 2011 at the Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai, where India faced Sri Lanka. Sri Lanka batted first and set a target of 275 runs, with Mahela Jayawardene scoring a brilliant century. India’s chase began cautiously but gained momentum with key contributions from Gautam Gambhir (97 runs) and a memorable 91 not out from Dhoni, who sealed the victory with a six, bringing India home by 6 wickets. This win marked India’s second Cricket World Cup title, 28 years after their first triumph in 1983. The 2011 victory was historic not only for the team but also for the nation, as it was Sachin Tendulkar’s first World Cup win in his sixth attempt, fulfilling a lifelong dream and delighting millions of fans. The tournament inspired a new generation of cricketers and strengthened India’s reputation as a dominant force in world cricket. It also highlighted the importance of teamwork, resilience, and leadership, particularly Dhoni’s calm and strategic captaincy, which played a pivotal role in India’s success.
